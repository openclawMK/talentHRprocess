# PeopleQuest Talent AI — What's New

A plain-language log of new features, most recent first. Share this with your
team so everyone knows what changed.

---

## 30 June 2026

Four updates this round — the headline is that you can now see, at a glance,
**how well each candidate matches your ideal hire**, send the personality test
to people who skipped it, and keep an eye on **salary expectations vs. budget**.

### 1. Success Profile fit — see how each candidate matches your "ideal hire" ⭐

Until now, scores told you how candidates compared *to each other*. Now you also
get a direct comparison **against the Success Profile you defined for the role**
(the must-haves, nice-to-haves, dealbreakers, ideal personality, and benchmarks).

On each candidate's page you'll see a new **Success Profile fit** card showing:

- A single **fit score (0–100%)** and a clear verdict — *Strong fit*, *Partial
  fit*, *Weak fit*, or *Dealbreaker*.
- A tick-list of your **must-haves** and **nice-to-haves** (met ✓ or missing ✗).
- A red alert if any **dealbreaker** is triggered.
- How they line up on your **experience / team-size benchmarks** and **ideal
  personality** traits.

This answers the question: *"Forget the other applicants — does this person
actually fit what we said we wanted?"*

> **Where:** open any candidate → the **Success Profile fit** card sits just
> below the score breakdown. (Make sure the role's Success Profile is filled in
> first — the button to do that is on the role's dashboard.)

### 2. Send the personality test to candidates who haven't done it 🧠

Some candidates (especially ones you added manually) won't have completed the
OCEAN personality questionnaire. You can now send it to them individually.

- On a candidate who's missing it, you'll see a **"Personality assessment not
  completed"** card.
- **Send via WhatsApp** delivers them a private link, or use **Copy link** to
  share it yourself.
- The candidate completes the short questionnaire on their phone — no login, no
  re-uploading their CV — and their results attach automatically, unlocking the
  personality part of their score.

> **Where:** open a candidate who hasn't done the questionnaire → the purple
> assessment card appears near the top.

### 3. Expected salary + budget fit 💰

You can now capture what a candidate expects to be paid and compare it to your
budget for the role — as a **separate budget signal that never affects their
quality score** (so a great candidate is never marked down just for their asking
price).

- **Candidates** now see an optional **"Expected monthly salary (RM)"** field on
  the application form.
- **You** can set a **salary budget range (min–max)** for each role in its
  Success Profile (the AI will even suggest a realistic range for you).
- Each candidate then shows a **budget chip**: *Within budget*, *Slightly above*,
  *Over budget*, *Below range*, or just their asking figure if no budget is set.
- It also appears in the **Compare** view so you can weigh affordability
  side-by-side.

> **Where:** the chip is on the candidate's header and inside the Success Profile
> fit card (clearly marked *"not counted in fit %"*). Set the budget range on the
> role's Success Profile page.

### 4. Small polish

- The **Success Profile** button on the role dashboard now has a soft violet
  colour instead of plain white, so it's easier to spot.

---

### A couple of things to note

- **Budget and personality links rely on a phone number / WhatsApp being set up.**
  If WhatsApp isn't connected yet, you'll still get a link to copy and share
  manually — nothing breaks.
- **Older candidates** added before today won't have an expected salary on file,
  so their budget chip simply won't show until that information is provided.
